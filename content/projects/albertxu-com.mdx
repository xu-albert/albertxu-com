## Background

I had a personal website on Squarespace since late 2024. It worked fine for what I needed: portfolio, resume, contact form. But I was paying $16/month for something I wasn't fully in control of, and I wanted more out of it.

Around the same time, I started applying for a Content Engineer role at Vercel. Having my own site running on the platform I was applying to work on seemed like the right move. I'd used Next.js on a previous project, so I wasn't starting from scratch.

Two motivations: save money and prove I could build on the platform.

## The migration

I pointed Claude at my existing Squarespace site for reference. Same color palette, similar layout, and visually accessible. Most of the content carried over as-is: the copy, the structure, the general feel. The new additions were a blog section and projects I'd worked on in the past year.

The technical build was the easy part. Getting things to look right was harder. Typography, spacing, hover states, the kind of details that don't show up in a feature list but make the difference between a site that feels finished and one that doesn't. Writing the website copy took far longer than setting up the website itself.

## Building the blog

I wanted a blog that supported MDX so I could write posts in Markdown with embedded React components. The feature I was most excited about was Mermaid diagram support. I could write a diagram in code and have it render as a flowchart or sequence diagram, without drawing anything by hand or screenshotting from other tools.

Claude Code with Superpowers helped a lot here. The skills and plugins were useful for deciding how the blog styling should look: prose typography, code blocks, spacing. Decisions that would have taken me a while to get right on my own were resolved quickly through iteration with Claude.

## How Claude helped

I want to be transparent about how much of this was AI-assisted. I used Claude for most of the implementation. I described what I wanted, and Claude wrote the code. My job was making decisions: what the site should look like, what content to include, how things should be organized.

The decisions were the hard part. The code was the easy part. Choosing the right words for the homepage took longer than setting up the entire Next.js project.

Migrating content from my old Squarespace site was also easier than expected. Claude could fetch the content from the old site directly and help restructure it for the new one.

## Timeline

The entire site went from `create-next-app` to deployed in about four days. Most of that time was spent on decision-making and copywriting, not coding. The technical setup (routing, MDX, contact form, deployment) came together quickly. The iteration on design and content is what took the bulk of the time.
